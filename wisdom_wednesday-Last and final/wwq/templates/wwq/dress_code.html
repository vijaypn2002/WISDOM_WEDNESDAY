Dress Code Policy

1. Applicability:

All employees of Indian Institute of Commerce Lakshya

2. Guidelines:

  • Monday to Friday: Business Formals
  • Saturday & Sunday: Business Casuals

2.1 Men

  • Business Formals – Trouser and collared shirt with formal shoes and belt.
  • Business Casual – T-shirts (Collared) with jeans. Round neck T-shirts with any slogans are not allowed - Sneakers/Sports shoes are allowed.
  • Footwear: Closed-toe formal shoes are allowed. Crocs, Sandals, or flip-flops are not allowed.

2.2 Women

  • Business Formals - Western Formals and Indian Formals also (Formal shirts, trousers & Kurthi’s are allowed). Sarees for women can also be considered appropriate.
  • Business Casuals – Jean with appropriate T-shirts (without any slogans) and tops. Sneakers/sports shoes are allowed.
  • Footwear: Closed-toe half shoes, and sandals are allowed. Crocs & flip-flops are not allowed.

3. Professionalism:

Emphasize the importance of maintaining a professional appearance that reflects positively on the institute.

4. Neat and Well-Groomed:

During all work hours, employees have to appear in a neat and professional manner. Employees are expected to be suitably attired, well-groomed, and ensure that attire is clean and in good repair. All employees have to maintain appropriate hygiene standards, to include but not limited to, excessive personal odor and/or fragrance, ensuring personal physical cleanliness, etc.

5. Other Important Aspects to Note:

  • Festival/Cultural attire is allowed only on authorized days based on officially declared festival/cultural dressing communication from the HR department.
  • It is mandatory to wear the business formals on official meetings or discussions.
  • Any exceptions to the business attire which are required for religious purposes or to honor cultural traditions need to be discussed with the HR Department.

6. Indiscipline and Breach Policy:

An employee found inappropriately dressed shall be asked to leave the office premises by his/her reporting manager/HRD to change their attire and return. In case the employee doesn’t return to work within a reasonable period of time, they will be marked absent for the day. Repeated instances will invoke disciplinary action.

Dress Code - Office Environment

Below are some general guidelines for both men and women regarding what attire is appropriate.

Clothing:

  • Mon - Fri: Formal Shirt/Trouser/Indian Wear/Business Suit. For Ladies: Kurthi/tops, Skirt/Saree/Indian Dress.
  • Inappropriate: Bicycle pants, Cargos Pants, Hoody, Sweatpants/Athletic Pants, Army Fatigues, Overalls, Low‐rise jeans or pants Jean.
  • Sat - Sun: Golf/Polo Shirts, T‐Shirts (without slogans or pictures), Dresses, Tops, suits and skirts. (Saturday & Sundays Only)
  • Inappropriate: Sweatshirts, Rugby Shirts, Mini Skirts, Ripped Jeans, Short dresses.

Shoes:

  • Male: Formal Black or Brown shoes (Leather/Suede) - Monday – Friday. Casual Shoes on Saturday and Sunday.
  • Female: Peep‐Toes, Closed Shoes, Buckled Scandal (Monday – Friday).
  • Inappropriate: Flip Flops/Slip‐On, Sneakers, Crocs, Sandals & Floaters.
